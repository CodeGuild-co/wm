The Challenge

There's a project element to this course, something that you will be working on throughout the week as individuals and in teams

A big part of a Software Engineer's CV is a record of work

Employers really like it if they can see evidence that you know what you're doing

A popular way to provide this evidence is through a blog

Developers write about lots of things:

  • Thoughts on software engineering
  • Solutions to problems they encountered
  • Languages and frameworks that they're using
  • Fun things, personal things, anything!

The communication skills you learn by writing a blog are also really good things to have on your CV

Being to communicate complicated topics well is a fantastic ability that employers will absolutely love

It's not just about employment though, writing a blog makes you part of a huge community of technical people who share ideas and learn new things

The challenge this week is to build your own blog and to start to write posts about computer science and software engineering

Each session that we do this week should teach you something new

After the session you should distil the things you've learned into a blog post

You might want to write posts that explain the topic you've just learned to others

You might want to write a memo to yourself so you can come back in the future

You might want to disagree with the contents of the session

You might write up a scathing review

It's up to you

The goal is that you start this pattern of learning something and writing about it

In order to make things interesting we're going to get into 6 groups

Each group will take a programming language and a web framework that they'll use to create their individual blogs

You'll work as a team to learn and explore your language and framework

You'll work individuall writing up blog posts on your own blog

At the end of the week, you'll present back to the room the things you've learned

The whole room is then going to vote for the language and framework that they think looks the best
